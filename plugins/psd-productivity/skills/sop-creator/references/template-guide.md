# PSD SOP Template & Convention Guide

## Official Template Structure

Every SOP uses these required sections (from the official PSD template):

```
Title: [Clear, descriptive title of the procedure]

Scope: [Who/what this applies to]

Procedure:
[Numbered steps with sub-steps]

Safety Considerations:
[Safety-related concerns — write "N/A" only if truly none]

Quality Control:
[Who monitors compliance, how, how often]

References:
[Board policies, RCWs, WACs, CBAs]

Revision History:
[Date - Author Initials]

 LH_swoop
```

## Optional Sections (add when needed)

These sections are NOT in the official template but appear in existing SOPs when complexity requires them. Insert them in this order:

| Section | Insert After | When to Use |
|---------|-------------|-------------|
| **Purpose** | Title | When the "why" isn't obvious from the title (Teaching & Learning dept uses this) |
| **Definitions** | Scope | When specialized terminology is used (3+ terms needing definition) |
| **Eligibility** | Within Procedure | When not all staff/students qualify |
| **Compliance** | After Procedure | When specific legal compliance requirements exist |
| **Contact** | After Quality Control | When a named person is the go-to (format: "Name, email@psd401.net") |
| **Addendum** | After Revision History | For severity charts, flowcharts, supplemental forms |
| **Glossary** | After Addendum | When disciplinary or legal terms need defining |

## Department-Specific Patterns

### Safety and Security
- Most procedural, step-by-step
- Substantive Safety Considerations (never N/A)
- Evidence handling language (case numbers, evidence bags, chain of custody)
- Law enforcement interaction protocols
- Uses Do's/Don'ts tables where helpful
- Emergency inventory lists with quantities

### Employee Support Services
- Two types: (a) policy-level with severity charts/flowcharts, (b) step-by-step Freshservice how-tos
- Severity classification: Minor / Moderate / Severe with table
- References Weingarten Rights, Loudermill rights
- Escalation paths: Building Principal → ESS/Paralegals → Legal/Superintendent
- Freshservice ticket workflow instructions

### Teaching and Learning
- Adds Purpose, Compliance, and Contact sections
- Tab-by-tab software walkthroughs (pdEnroller, RedRover)
- Qualifying vs non-qualifying examples
- References WAC 181-85 (clock hours), CCDEI/SEL standards
- Naming conventions for system entries

### Athletics & Activities

## Writing Conventions

### Voice and Tone
- Direct, imperative language: "The principal will...", "Staff must...", "Navigate to..."
- Professional but accessible — written for practitioners, not lawyers
- Active voice preferred
- Present tense for procedures

### Scope Phrasing
Use exact audience descriptions:
- "All PSD employees and volunteers"
- "All schools / Office Managers"
- "Building Administrators, Counselors, Teachers"
- "This applies to all district employees, students, and by extension, families and the community"

### Procedure Formatting
- **Numbered steps** (1, 2, 3...) for sequential procedures
- **Lettered sub-steps** (a, b, c) or **sub-numbers** (1a, 1b, 2a) for branching within steps
- **Bullet points** for lists within steps
- **Bold text** for emphasis on critical actions
- Use "STEP 1, STEP 2, STEP 3" format for simple 3-step processes
- For complex SOPs, use Roman numerals for major sections (I, II, III) with numbered steps within

### Tables
Use tables for:
- Severity/classification charts (Minor/Moderate/Severe)
- Activity type → funding source mappings
- Role → responsibility assignments
- Timeline/schedule grids
- Inventory lists with quantities

### Revision History Format
```
Date - Author Initials
```
Examples from existing SOPs:
- `08/09/2024 - KH`
- `Created: 12/27/23 - MK`
- `October 2025`
- `Dec 2025 / Feb 2026`

## Example SOP: Simple (Reporting a Suspicious Vehicle)

```
Title: Reporting a Suspicious Vehicle

Scope: All PSD employees and volunteers

Procedure:
1. Document the vehicle details (make, model, color, license plate)
2. Note the location, time, and any occupant descriptions
3. Report to building administration immediately
4. Administration contacts School Safety Officers (SSOs)
5. SSOs assess and contact PCSO if warranted
6. Document in Freshservice ticket system

Safety Considerations:
Do not approach the vehicle. Maintain a safe distance and observe from inside the building when possible. Personal safety takes priority over information gathering.

Quality Control:
SSOs maintain a database of reported suspicious vehicles. Reports are reviewed monthly for patterns.

References:

Revision History:
[Date] - [Initials]

 LH_swoop
```

## Example SOP: Moderate (Fingerprinting)

```
Title: SOP - Fingerprinting

Scope: All new hires, volunteers with unsupervised student access, and contractors

Procedure:
1. New hire or volunteer identified as requiring fingerprinting
   a. New certificated/classified employees — full fingerprint required
   b. Substitutes — full fingerprint required
   c. Volunteers with unsupervised access — full fingerprint required
   d. Contractors — WATCH report minimum; full fingerprint if extended access
2. ESS initiates fingerprint request through approved vendor
3. Results reviewed by HR/ESS
4. Clearance documented in employee file
5. If adverse findings, escalate to Background Review SOP

Safety Considerations:
No individual may have unsupervised access to students without completed and cleared background check.

Quality Control:
ESS Technicians audit clearance records quarterly. Board policy 5100 compliance verified during annual review.

References:
RCW 28A.400.303
Board Policy 5100

Revision History:
[Date] - [Initials]

 LH_swoop
```
